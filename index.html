Calorie Deficit & Macronutrient Calculation
Step-by-step calculation using Katch-McArdle (lean-mass-based) formula
⚠ Why Katch-McArdle, not Mifflin-St Jeor Generic BMR calculators assume average body composition. At 30% BF, they overestimate BMR by 150–200 kcal/day. Using lean body mass gives a more honest number. Both methods are run below — they converge, confirming accuracy.

Step 1 — Body Composition

Weight = 116 kg | Body Fat = 30%
Fat Mass = 116 × 0.30 = 34.8 kg
Lean Body Mass = 116 − 34.8 = 81.2 kg

Step 2 — BMR (Katch-McArdle)

BMR = 370 + (21.6 × LBM)
= 370 + (21.6 × 81.2)
= 370 + 1,753.92
= 2,124 kcal / day

Cross-check (Mifflin-St Jeor):
= (10×116) + (6.25×187) − (5×41) + 5
= 1,160 + 1,168.75 − 205 + 5
= 2,128 kcal / day ← ≈ 0.2% delta. Confirmed.

After 8 PM Hunger Protocol — 14 Days
Dinner 7 PM · Bedtime 10 PM · Trigger food: peanut butter
⚠ Critical reframe Dinner at 7 PM, bed at 10 PM — that's a 3-hour gap while you're in a caloric deficit. Some of this hunger is physiologically real, not emotional weakness. Treating it as pure willpower will fail. This protocol handles both the physical and behavioral components.

3 Questions — Ask Before Entering the Kitchen

Question 1

"On a scale of 1–10, how physically hungry am I?"

Stomach growling, lightheadedness, hollow feeling = physical. Restlessness, boredom, wanting a specific taste = behavioral. Score below 6? Proceed to Layer 1 — it's not real hunger.

Question 2

"What has happened since dinner that might be driving this?"

Name the trigger: screen time, stress, boredom, habit, nothing to do. Name it out loud or write it. This alone reduces craving intensity by making the behavior conscious.

Question 3

"Would I eat plain boiled chicken and broccoli if that's all there was?"

Yes → genuinely hungry. Proceed to Layer 3 if Layers 1 and 2 failed.
No → it's a craving. Stay in Layers 1–2.

The 3-Layer Defense

1
5-Minute Delay — Do One of These First
  • Brush teeth immediately — Mint taste disrupts craving signals and creates a "kitchen closed" psychological cue
  • 20 slow bodyweight squats — Shifts blood flow and breaks the mental pattern driving you toward food
  • Write or voice-memo why you want it — "I'm about to eat because ___" makes the behavior conscious
  • 300 ml cold water — Volume effect, temperature disrupts the craving cycle
2
Zero-Calorie Suppressors — If Still Hungry After 5 Min
  • Peppermint or chamomile tea (no honey) — suppresses appetite, aids sleep
  • Sparkling water with squeeze of lemon or lime
  • Sugar-free chewing gum for 5–10 minutes
  • Iced still water (volume + cold sensation)

Sleep & Metabolism Optimization
Bedtime 10 PM · Wake 6 AM · Current issue: screen time before bed
⚠ Screen time is silently sabotaging your fat loss Certain Blue light suppresses melatonin by up to 2 hours. Delayed melatonin means elevated evening cortisol. Elevated cortisol at night = fat storage, particularly visceral. Combined with the hunger hormones below, poor sleep quality may be responsible for 0.2–0.4 kg/week of "invisible" fat loss being blocked.

The 3 Hormones That Control Overnight Fat Loss

Hour-by-Hour Evening Protocol

7:00 PM
Dinner — highest protein meal of the day. Finish eating here. The night hunger protocol from Tab 3 handles anything after this point.
7:30 PM
10–15 minute light walk. Not intense — digestion aid, mild post-meal blood sugar management, and gentle cortisol reduction. This one habit alone improves next-day fat oxidation.
8:00 PM
Prep tomorrow. Lay out gym clothes, pre-weigh and pack food, write tomorrow's schedule. This reduces morning cortisol spike and decision fatigue — both of which affect eating behavior.
8:30 PM
Screen transition. Switch to night mode on all screens OR switch to no-screen activity (physical book, podcast without screen, light stretching). This is your hard blue-light cutoff.
9:00 PM
Wind-down. Reading, gentle stretching (10 min), journaling. No news, no emotionally stimulating content. Chamomile or valerian tea optional — both have mild evidence for sleep onset improvement.
9:30 PM
Bedroom prep. Room temperature: 18–20°C is optimal for deep sleep. Phone charging outside the bedroom or face-down on Do Not Disturb. Blackout curtains if available.
9:45 PM
No screens — final 15 min. Dark, quiet, minimal stimulation. 4-7-8 breathing (inhale 4s, hold 7s, exhale 8s) if restless. This alone can cut sleep onset time by 10–15 min.
10:00 PM
Sleep target. 8 hours → natural wake at 6 AM. Consistent bedtime is more important than total hours.

3 Reasons for the Current Plateau

01

Calorie creep inside OMAD Certain

A single large meal has no natural stopping cue the way multiple meals do. One home-cooked Indian meal — dal + rice + chicken + two chapatis + yogurt + cooking oil measured by eye — easily reaches 1,800–2,200 kcal. Add any evening snacking or drinks and you hit 2,800–3,100 kcal without realizing. OMAD doesn't make calories disappear; it makes them harder to see.

02

Metabolic adaptation — reduced TDEE Likely

17.5 kg lighter = lower energy cost to exist and move. Your TDEE has dropped by approximately 150–250 kcal since you started. The eating pattern that produced a deficit 30 weeks ago now produces a smaller deficit or none at all. This is expected biology — not failure. The target needs recalibration (done in Tab 1: 2,300 kcal).

03

NEAT reduction — unconscious movement decrease Guessing

Under prolonged caloric deficit, the body unconsciously reduces non-exercise movement — fidgeting, standing frequency, walking pace, stair use. You may be burning 200–400 kcal less per day in NEAT than 6 months ago without any conscious change. This is rarely tracked but contributes meaningfully to plateaus.
