After 8 PM Hunger Protocol — 14 Days
Dinner 7 PM · Bedtime 10 PM · Trigger food: peanut butter
⚠ Critical reframe Dinner at 7 PM, bed at 10 PM — that's a 3-hour gap while you're in a caloric deficit. Some of this hunger is physiologically real, not emotional weakness. Treating it as pure willpower will fail. This protocol handles both the physical and behavioral components.

3 Questions — Ask Before Entering the Kitchen

Question 1

"On a scale of 1–10, how physically hungry am I?"

Stomach growling, lightheadedness, hollow feeling = physical. Restlessness, boredom, wanting a specific taste = behavioral. Score below 6? Proceed to Layer 1 — it's not real hunger.

Question 2

"What has happened since dinner that might be driving this?"

Name the trigger: screen time, stress, boredom, habit, nothing to do. Name it out loud or write it. This alone reduces craving intensity by making the behavior conscious.

Question 3

"Would I eat plain boiled chicken and broccoli if that's all there was?"

Yes → genuinely hungry. Proceed to Layer 3 if Layers 1 and 2 failed.
No → it's a craving. Stay in Layers 1–2.

The 3-Layer Defense

1
5-Minute Delay — Do One of These First
  • Brush teeth immediately — Mint taste disrupts craving signals and creates a "kitchen closed" psychological cue
  • 20 slow bodyweight squats — Shifts blood flow and breaks the mental pattern driving you toward food
  • Write or voice-memo why you want it — "I'm about to eat because ___" makes the behavior conscious
  • 300 ml cold water — Volume effect, temperature disrupts the craving cycle
2
Zero-Calorie Suppressors — If Still Hungry After 5 Min
  • Peppermint or chamomile tea (no honey) — suppresses appetite, aids sleep
  • Sparkling water with squeeze of lemon or lime
  • Sugar-free chewing gum for 5–10 minutes
  • Iced still water (volume + cold sensation)

Trigger: boredom · Peak time: 8 PM onward · Missing most: chips, sweets
What's actually driving this Likely Evening cravings are dopamine-seeking behavior in unstructured time. After a day of constraint — office, diet, routine — your brain hits an unstructured window at 8 PM and demands a reward. Food (especially sweet/salty) is the fastest, cheapest dopamine hit available. You're not weak. You're running a neurological pattern that responds to behavioral interventions, not willpower.
